College is meant to be one of the most transformative experiences of our lives.
When I look back on my college experience, I think of it fondly.
But it makes me wonder what I would have done differently or what others might have to say.

Flashback to May of 2016. I had just finished my freshman year of college and I was heading into my sophomore year at the University of Pennsylvania. At this point, I wasn't completely sure what I wanted to do with my life. I was on the pre-medical track and thought of attending medical school after graduation, but it wasn't something I could really see myself doing. I was drawn more towards my interests in the fine arts, especially English and creative writing. I would have loved to explore my interests in the fine arts a bit more while I was at school but I became so preoccupied with trying to do well in my pre-medical coursework that I didn't take advantage of the opportunity to pursue potentially more meaningful

But in the fall of 2016 leading up into my junior year I did try a few things

  • I took a writing seminar on memory and cognition and an introduction to 17th C lit with a Milton class.
  • I continued my premed coursework and finished up my Calculus sequence along with Biology.
  • I started taking intermediate Biology courses and worked on finishing my Chemistry requirement
  • I took an interesting class on Modern Britain and read a range of British literature from 1700-2015

During my junior year, I started taking more courses in the English major but I didn't feel like the ones I were taking were quite right for me. I took the following courses in the fall:

  1. Topics in Romanticism: Wordsworth and Coleridge
  2. Topics Drama to 1660: Intro to Shakespeare
  3. Modern American Lit: Virginia Woolf
  4. The World of Work

The classes were too theory-based for me and I was not interested in the academic side of English literature. It's good to know a bit of theory as a writer but the creative writing classes I'd take later would incorporate just the right amount of theory into workshop time in a way that felt more natural for understanding the writing process.

Now, speaking of creative writing, here's what I took in the spring:

  1. Photographs and Stories: Writing from Photographs
  2. Documentary Writing
  3. Dante's Divine Comedy
  4. Elementary French I
  5. Comparative Vertebrate Anatomy and Evolution
  6. Comparative Vertebrate Anatomy and Evolution Lab (6to9pm!!!!)
  7. Evolutionary Biology

    8. This was a heavy semester when it came to labs and things like that and I regret taking classes that I didn't feel I truly enjoyed simply because I was afraid of career prospects. Instead of exploring things like tech and design, I kept pushing myself towards courses in Biology and the natural sciences because it just felt like that's what I was being pressured to do.

    If I could go back in time, I would have built upon my creative writing experience throughout my senior year. During my senior year, I packed on more science classes and a few electives I needed to take. Post-graduation, I continued my education in the natural sciences and was about to apply to med school when the little voice inside of me started telling me to run...
